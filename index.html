How this works

The weekly loop that runs this whole thing.

Step 1 — Research

Find 10-15 profiles a week at companies you care about. Add each as a contact in the Pipeline tab with their name, company, title, and LinkedIn URL. Set status to Researching.

Step 2 — Draft

Paste what you know about them (bio, recent posts, career path) into the Generate tab. Pick the note that sounds most like you and send it to the pipeline — it lands in that contact's notes, ready to copy.

Step 3 — Send

Open LinkedIn and send the connection request manually, pasting the generated note. Cap yourself at 15-20 sends per day — this keeps your account safe and matches LinkedIn's own soft limits.

Mark the contact Sent and fill in the date.

Step 4 — Sync weekly

Once a week, open My Network → Connections, sorted by date added. For anyone who accepted, mark them Accepted in the pipeline.

Step 5 — Conversation
  • First message (1-2 days after acceptance): thank them for connecting, ask one specific, easy-to-answer question about their path or role.
  • Follow-ups (over weeks, only if they reply): follow their lead — ask about their day-to-day, advice for your major, share your own projects only if relevant.
  • Referral ask (3-6 weeks in, only after real conversation): "I've really enjoyed our conversations — I'm applying for Summer 2027 internships and [Company] is high on my list. Would you be comfortable referring me, or pointing me to the right person? No worries at all if not!"
Safety
  • Never use bots or browser extensions to send connections or messages — this is the fastest way to get permanently banned.
  • Stay under 15-20 new requests per day.
  • Keep notes under 300 characters — this app flags it for you automatically.
  • All your data lives in this browser only (localStorage). Nothing is sent anywhere except the note-generation request to Claude's API.
